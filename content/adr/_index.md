An Architectural Decision Record (ADR) is a document that captures an important architectural decision made along with its context and consequences.
The records are resource for understanding the rationale behind the design. Maintaining a historical log also assists in future architectural decisions.
ADRs provide a structured and efficient way to capture and communicate important architectural decisions, ensuring they add value to the project and its stakeholders.

Minimum Components of an ADR:

- **Title**: A concise name that reflects the decision being made.
- **Status**: The current status of the decision (e.g., proposed, accepted, rejected, superseded).
- **Context**: Capture the relevant factors and motivations that influenced the decision-making process.
- **Decision**: A statement of the final decision.
- **Consequences**: The impact of the decision, including the benefits and potential drawbacks.
